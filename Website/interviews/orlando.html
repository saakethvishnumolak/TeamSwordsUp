Orlando Roblero

Athlete on Bellarmine's sprint football team.

Why did you start playing Sprint Football? What inspired or motivated you to play?

  • Talked to a friend who was managing the team - encouraged to talk to the coach, and soon got an offer to practice with the team.
  • New experience - has experience with soccer, but soon found himself liking the team

How large of a role is Sprint Football in your life?

  • On a weekly basis - 2 hrs a day, 2 days a week - lifts.
  • Half the time on campus, not including school and social stuff.

What’s the hardest part of your job as a player?

  • Showing up and being confident.
  • Can easily perform well as a team, but without confidence, individual performance is lacking.

What is your favorite part of being a collegiate athlete? What aspects or tasks within the job do you wish you could devote more of your time and energy?

  • Meeting new people - being on a team and meeting similar people.
  • Connections - bonds, family like with teammates.
  • Wish had more time to working on new skills - listen to memo for details.

What program or tool do you or your team currently use to keep track of your performance statistics?

  • Students record it - “Film” (Tactic)
  • Huddle - App
  • Before or after practice - look into individual stats and jot it down

Why did you choose this tool? What stands out about this method compared to others?

  • Familiar - used in high school, and players typically keep it throughout college.
  • Keeps a record of high school stats - incredibly useful for trying out for college teams.

What’s something about this tool that you wish could be improved? What are its current shortcomings?

  • Not a frequent user - position doesn’t really require statistics to be heavily tracked.
  • Huddle is fine - coaches do most of the monitoring, and makes judgements based off of data on how to improve.
