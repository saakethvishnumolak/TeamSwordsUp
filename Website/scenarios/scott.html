Lily Scott

Scenario One - Extra Work After Practice

Lily is in her second year as the head coach of the girls’ volleyball team in a small high school outside of Lexington. They are three weeks into practice and have their first game coming up in a week and a half. After practice on Friday, a girl came up to Lily and asked what she had to work on to get more playing time.

The girl was a junior, so she already had some stats from the previous year. Lily went into the history logs and checked her stats and saw what she was lacking in. She sees that the girl needs to improve her dig.

Lily sets up a drill for the girl and has her assistant coach film to check for form. Lily puts all the videos into the app which allows the girl to go back later and see what she needs to work on regarding her form.


User Stories

  • As a coach, I want to be able to check my players stats from previous years so I can see where they need to improve.
  • As a coach, I want to be able to do drills for each player depending on the areas they need to improve on so they can perform better.
  • As a coach, I want to be able to upload video clips of players during practice so my players can review them and improve their technique.
  • As a player and a visual learner, I want to be able to view videos to better improve my weaknesses so I can get more playing time.
  • As a player, I want to be able to receive constructive criticism through the app so that I know what to work on in practice.
  • As a coach, I want to give feedback on a player's stats or video so that they know exactly what they need to improve on.
  • As a coach, I want to be able to analyze the trends in a player's performance over time so I can coach accordingly with them.
  • As a coach, I want to be able to share the player's stats and performance data so that they stay motivated and encouraged to compete.

Scenario Two - Scout Team

Lily has gotten her team off to a very hot start. They have been winning a lot of games but not by much. They have their toughest opponent yet this coming Friday. Lily wants to use the app to scout the opponents line up. She watched the previous games and recorded the players’ season stats. She used them to create a projected lineup of who she thinks is going to start. She also made some notes about some bench players that have made an impact this year.

Starting that Monday leading up to the game, she pulled some of the bench players to the side and asked them to act as the scout team. The scout team played exactly like their opponents to emulate what they might do against them on Friday.


User Stories

  • As a coach, I want to utilize the app to research opponent lineups and their stats in order to better prepare my players for future games.
  • As a coach, I want to compile and examine the stats of the opposing players to estimate a lineup and plan appropriately.
  • As a coach, I want to develop a scout team among my players that will mimic the playing style of the opposition so they can be better prepared for the game. 
  • As a coach, I want to keep track of any opposing bench players that can have a big impact on the game so that my players will be prepared for what to expect when they come into the game. 
  • As a coach, I would like to keep expected opponent lineups in the app so that I may use them as a resource while planning game strategies.
  • As a coach, I want to use the app to communicate my scouting notes and reports with my team so they are well aware of the opposition.
  • As a coach, I want to identify and emphasize specific opponents' team members who could have a big impact on the game so that my players are informed and ready.
  • As a coach, I want to use the app's archived game footage to assess our opponents' tactics and prepare our own counterattack.

Scenario Three - Midpoint Shakeup

Lily’s team is halfway through the season. They are coming up against the second ranked team in their division. Lily has run into a little dilemma this week as she is struggling with matchup problems. She was checking the opponents’ players stats on the app and found out that their best player, a top ranked center, has been dominating the competition. She checked her starting center’s stats and saw that she was not going to be a match against the other girl.

The following week of practices, Lily experimented with different lineups to see which best matches up with their opponent that week. She found that a center on the bench matched better for the upcoming game and figured out a rotation that worked for her and the team.


User Stories

  • As a coach, I want to compare the stats of my players with those of the opponents so I can find matchup issues and make wiser lineup selections.
  • As a coach, I want to test out several lineups throughout practice so I can get the best lineup for the upcoming game.
  • As a coach, I want to assess how the bench players are doing so that I can identify any prospective matchups that may be advantageous in the next game.
  • As a coach, I want to optimize our performance and chances of victory against difficult opponents by adjusting squad rotations based on looking at the matchups.
  • As a coach, I want to evaluate the matches by comparing player statistics with those of the players on the other team.
  • As a coach, I want to record and evaluate various lineups during practice so that I can make data-driven judgments for the real game.
  • As a coach, I want to get input from my players in the app on various lineups and tactics to make sure they feel at ease and in control.
  • As a coach, I want to keep an eye on how players who have been shifted to different roles are doing to make sure they are adjusting well and getting better.
