Scenario One

After a training session, a player comes up to Eric asking if he could train him. Eric, like with all the new players gives him a free session and then also watches him play live beforehand to see what he is dealing with. He then checks the app to see his past stats and performances. This player played shortstop and he committed way too many errors over the past couple seasons.

Eric puts the player through multiple drills. Hitting ground balls and fast line outs to him to increase reaction time and technique. He then moves on to improve his throwing accuracy from shortstop to second and first base. He tracks progress as the season and training sessions continue to see how the player improves.


User Stories

  • As a trainer, I want to arrange players' training sessions using the app so that I can manage my time and the players receive the attention they require.
  • As a trainer, I want to check my players' previous numbers and performances so I can identify their strengths and weaknesses.
  • As a trainer, I want to create unique drills through the app so I can focus on particular areas where my players need to grow.
  • As a trainer, I want to record my players' progress in the app so I can track my trainees' growth over time.
  • As a trainer, I want to check player mistake tendencies so I can create specialized training plans.
  • As a trainer, I want to monitor players' reaction times throughout drills so I can help player become faster for games.
  • As a trainer, I want to develop training exercises that will assist players throw more accurately from particular field positions.
  • As a trainer, I want to use the app to communicate progress updates with my players so they stay informed and motivated.

Scenario Two

A standout Pitcher is recovering from Tommy John which he suffered a year ago. He was finally cleared and came to Eric to help get his velocity back. Eric begins by easing the pitcher back into things. This starts with a lot of plyometrics and hypertrophy work with a lot of band/resistance work. Add in cardio to get back in shape. Then the player will start throwing after a couple of weeks.

Eric makes the pitcher use a wearable to track the pitcher’s mechanics. See what to tweak to fix the throwing form and ultimately increase the velocity. Eric uses the app to put all this information in. He can then see trends and patterns which allow him to make data driven adjustments to the training moving forward.


User Stories

  • As a trainer, I want to develop rehab plans for those coming back from injury via the app.
  • As a trainer, I want to monitor the players' recovery process in order to modify the plan as necessary and guarantee a secure recovery.
  • As a trainer, I want incorporate wearables with the app to track player's mechanics so I can collect the data to analyze later.
  • As a trainer, I want to examine how pitchers throw in order to assist them develop better techniques and increase velocity.
  • As a trainer, I want to design plyometric and resistance training programs in the app so it can help build players' strength and mobility.
  • As a trainer, I want to track players' cardiovascular fitness so they are in shape for the game
  • As a trainer, I want to find trends and patterns in the training data of my players so I can make data-driven changes to players training.
  • I want to create velocity targets for pitchers and track how they do in to achieving these goals as a coach.

Scenario Three

Eric has been training his player, Jake, for almost 3 months. After checking his stats on the app Eric saw that his batting average and on base percentage had not improved in that time. Eric opens the app and checks out some of the video clips to see what is going on. He quickly finds out that the player’s mechanics needed to change.

Next training session they start from the beginning. Eric had Jake wear a wearable and made him swing at a ball off a tee. Then he transfers the information from the wearable to the app. Eric then uses that to see the players’ bat swing/bat pattern and from there they start tweaking the swing.

A couple weeks later, Eric has the pitcher recovering from tommy john to throw live BP to Jake. This allows Eric to see how Jake will do in a controlled game like environment. From there they see what else needs to be changed. Eric transfers all the data into the app for Jake to go back later and review.


User Stories

  • As a trainer, I want to monitor my player's performance metrics on the app so I can evaluate their development over time.
  • As a trainer, I want to access and watch video footage of my players performances, so I can pinpoint areas of improvements.
  • As a trainer, I want to use the app to assess the mechanics of my athletes so I can create personalized training schedules.
  • As a trainer, I want my players to wear wearables so I can get info about my players' movements for in-depth analysis.
  • As a trainer, I want to be able to quickly move data from wearables to the app so I can review and analyze the data.
  • As a trainer, I want to use the app to evaluate bat swing patters so I can advise my athletes on how to improve their swing.
  • As a trainer, I want to conduct controlled training sessions, simulating game-like conditions so I can evaluate players' readiness and performance.
  • As a player, I want to examine the information and criticism from practice sessions on the app so I can see how I did and make any necessary adjustments.
